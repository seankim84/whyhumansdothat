Grounded in real research

This isn't self-help. It's psychology.

The program uses four well-known studies as training lenses — ways to test small behaviors and compare your fear with the evidence.

  • Aronson, 1966 — the pratfall effect: why a competent person's small mistake can make them more likable
  • Aron, 1997 — how strangers became close in 45 minutes through gradual, honest disclosure
  • Bruk, 2018 — the "beautiful mess effect": you judge your own vulnerability far more harshly than others do
  • Boothby, 2018 — the "liking gap": people underestimate how much others actually like them
